Sougata Sen

I am currently a postdoctoral scholar at Northwestern University, working with Prof. Nabil Alshurafa and Prof. Josiah Hester. Prior to joining Northwestern Universty, I was a postdoctoral scholar at Dartmouth College, working with Prof. David Kotz in topics related to mHealth security and privacy. I was involved in the THaW project. I earned my Ph.D. in 2017 from the School of Information Systems(SIS) at Singapore Management University (SMU), where I was supervised by Prof. Archan Misra. My Ph.D. thesis was titled "Fusing mobile, wearable and infrastructure sensing for immersive daily lifestyle analytics". In addition to academic research, I also have more than 6 years of industry research experience, where I was working on topics in the Wireless Sensor Network domain.

My research interest includes: Mobile and Wearable computing; Pervasive and Ubiquitous computing; IoT systems and applications; Mobile health;
